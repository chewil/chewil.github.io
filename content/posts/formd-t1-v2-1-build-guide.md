# The Ultimate Assembly Guide: Building in the FormD T1 v2.1

Setting up a Small Form Factor (SFF) case like the [FormD T1 v2.1](https://formdt1.com/products/t1black) is more of a "mechanical project" than a standard PC build. Because the case arrives in flat-packed pieces, you have to assemble the chassis itself before you even start putting your PC parts inside.

It requires some patience, but the result is one of the most premium, space-efficient 9.95L builds on the market. Here is the best way to approach the assembly, along with official resources and community tips.

## 1. Gather Your Resources

The minimalist instructions in the box can be tricky to decipher, so it helps to lean on both official and community guides.

* **Download the Official Manual:** Head to the [FormD Resources Page](https://formdt1.com/pages/resources) and download the **T1 V2.1 Assembly Manual** PDF.
* **Watch a Video Guide (Highly Recommended):** Visual aids are crucial for SFF builds. Check out the [FormD T1 V2 Sandwich AIO Build Guide](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=ivMawWusdZI) for a thorough step-by-step walkthrough covering parts, screwing guides, and full assembly.

## 2. Crucial Setup Tips Before You Start

Based on community feedback from builders, keep these specific quirks in mind before turning a single screw:

* **Sort Your Screws First:** The V2.1 utilizes 3 different types of M3 screws (countersunk and flat head) and 5 different spacers/standoffs. They often come unsorted. Take 5 minutes to separate and organize them! Using a countersunk screw where a flat head is required can strip the aluminum threads.
* **Determine Your Spacing:** Because it's a sandwich-style case, you must decide how much room to allocate to the CPU cooler versus the GPU. Know your GPU's exact slot width (e.g., 2-slot, 2.75-slot, or 3.25-slot) before locking in the central spine.
* **Watch the GPU Ports:** If you are building with a massive GPU (like a 4080/4090), you may need to temporarily remove the spine holder near the GPU IO ports to avoid scratching the card as you maneuver it in. 

## 3. The Recommended Assembly Order

While the official manual provides the engineering specs, most builders find this order the most logical:

1. **The Spine & Stand-offs:** Attach the motherboard standoffs to the central spine. This is the "backbone" of your build.
2. **Front & Rear Panels:** Bolt the front and rear panels to the spine. This creates the basic frame.
3. **The GPU Support:** Install the adjustable GPU strut. Move the spine to accommodate your specific GPU thickness.
4. **Bottom Panel:** Attach the bottom panel to solidify the structure.
5. **Component Installation:** Install your Motherboard, PSU, and GPU *before* putting on the side and top panels.
6. **The "Click" Finish:** When attaching the top and bottom panels, pinch the mesh side panels completely shut before inserting them into the front panel. This ensures everything seats flush and secures properly with the rear thumbscrews.

## 4. Let's Talk Cables

Cabling in a 9.95L case requires some planning. Here is what you need to know about powering your T1 build:

* **The Internal Power Extension:** The case comes with a **C13-C14 Inlet Cable**. This routes from the back panel plug to your PSU mounted deep inside. *Pro-tip: Route this cable under the spine before you install the motherboard.*
* **PSU to Component Cables:** You will use the cables that come in the box with your SFX Power Supply (like the Corsair SF750). *Note: The case does not include PSU mounting screws; use the ones that came with your power supply.*
* **Custom Cables (Highly Recommended):** While you *can* use stock PSU cables, they are often much too long for the T1. Ordering custom-length, unsleeved silicone cables (from vendors like CableMod or MODDIY) prevents cable clutter from blocking your airflow.

Take your time, keep your screws organized, and enjoy the process of building one of the cleanest SFF cases available!
